ABOUT MY WORK

Identity is a shifting construct of environment and context. I re-examine my own personal identity and belonging by confronting culture, history and traditions around me within the confines of painting the fa√ßade, or portraits of people. In relation to appropriating the image of persons from my private life and those in mainstream culture, I also perceive nameless faces I encounter in the everyday that comment on historical, ethnic and cross-cultural paths that have navigated people and communities over time. The paintings become reflections of current identity and analysis while considering shifting archetypes and prevailing narratives in the greater context of society. Presented in abstracted large-scale form, viewers are compelled to confront the paintings filtered through their own individual identities and experiences befittingly to participate and enter into the sublime dialogue and space between artist and subject.
